Light Clinic

Prescription Strength Digital Relaxation



Read About It

“Light Clinic” is a prescription strength relaxation venue designed to facilitate integration and intention setting against the backdrop of a city and a world experiencing springtime after a very long and destructive winter of plague and political unrest. Users begin by filling out an intake form. It will not only gather information about a patient’s proclivities and state of mind but also give them an opportunity to relax, breathe, and reflect. The intake form experience will be a part of the entire experience, and will make waiting in line an interesting experience on its own. In addition to filling out the form, patients can make use of their waiting time to explore a gallery of hypnotic, oddly satisfying art displayed on screens printed out in frames. Based on a user’s answers they will be “prescribed” a combination of experiences to stimulate the senses: visual, aural, vibrational, and olfactory. The first part of the experience will involve aromatherapy provided by “Shocks of Love”, a scent studio formerly stationed at Luxury Escapism. Their “prescription strength” smells can be applied to paper masks which can be given away for use on their own or on the outside of other’s masks. The second experience will involve the use of headphones, patients can plug in their own or use provided headphones which can be wiped down between uses. They will have a 5-10 minute ASMR sound journey inspired by “Sonic Sauna” by Matt McCorkle which premiered at Luxury Escapism. Users will then be led to vibroacoustic beds which vibrate softly as they meditate inside the worlds of “Visitations”. As an alternative, users who report wanting a more active experience and enough experience with VR will be led to a “Cosmic Sugar” station where they can experience flow states by playing with millions of particles. Patients who can confirm that they are not prone to epilepsy can take part in “Rainbow Therapy”, an experience of pure strobing lights and binaural beats. One on one sessions will be provided by Jacob Marshall as a part of “Greenwood Butterflies”, a multisensory experience that includes the timed use of fans, heat and Palo Santo incense. A networked experience called “Virtual Dome” places groups inside the same virtual space where they can paint mandalas together. Upon exiting, users will have an opportunity to push a button and record a wish. As the festival wraps, the wishes will be collected and burned, their ashes scattered in the waters near the World Trade Center in memory of the lives lost 20 years ago. All of these experiences have been tried and tested in location based settings but have never been brought together in this way before. The experience is modular and can be modified for on-site, outdoor, or at-home use - or a combination of all of these.
